Top 10 Old PC Games That Are Still Worth Playing

The list of modern games is endless and this never-ending list of games offer fantastic graphics and immersive gameplay. But there are several old games that are still special and worth playing. Playing the old classic games will not only fill you up with nostalgia but also will refresh your old memories related to the game and those old days. Here we have picked up 10 old PC games that must still seek out and play.

  1. StarCraft

    StarCraft is a real-time strategy game based on military science fiction. Published in 1998 by Blizzard Entertainment, StarCraft became the first game of the video game series. A number of modern games are not taking inspiration from strategies and factions of StarCraft and this makes it more worthy to play.

  2. Quake

    Quake is one of the best first-person shooters games of all time. There are four world's where you will find different types of horrific beasts and grunt to the terror. You have to fight through the four worlds in order to win the game.

  3. Grim Fandango

    The revolutionary adventure game Grim Fandango was developed by LucasArts and was a step forward in order to achieve the vision of LucasArts of bringing the adventure genre into the modern era of those days. The game has pre-rendered 3D environments which attracted a lot of players and the soundtrack is one of the best soundtracks.

  4. System Shock 2

    System Shock 2 is one of the best role-playing horror-survival games released in 1999. In System Shock 2, you are sent to space to check out for a mysterious distress call which was coming from a distance space sector. The game offers immersive soundtrack along with excellent storyline which also includes a few jumps and scares along the way.

  5. Theme Hospital

    The Business simulation game, Theme Hospital was published by Electronic Arts in 1997. The game has a campaign mode where you have to take control of hospitals and compete against other healthcare facilities in the same locality.

  6. Baldur's Gate II

    In the pantheon of RPG classics, Baldur's Gate II is one of the best RPGs. The game has a wonderfully intricate level design which sets it aside from the other games. It is a game of Dungeons and Dragons but the approach to its storyline and character makes it exceptional.

  7. OpenTTD

    OpenTTD is a game of business simulation. The players try to make money by transporting passengers and goods through rails, roadways, waterways or airways. The game offers you custom train sets, user-made building and stations, advanced signals, entire industry overhaul and much more.

  8. Fallout 2

    Fallout 2 is a post-nuclear role-playing game which offers turn-based role-playing. The game was developed by Black Isle Studios and was published in 1998 by Interplay productions. Character optimization is one of the enduring features of the Fallout series. The game has incredible depth.

  9. Half-Life

    Half-Life is widely regarded as one of the best games ever. It is based on the story of Dr Gordon Freeman. You are stuck into a Black Mesa Facility and you need to find your way back by solving puzzles and attempt to escape. The game was an incredible experience when it was released.

  10. Thief

    The first person classic stealth game, Thief is one of the first games in the genre of stealth which had a proper soundtrack and great environment. The game has several levels to move through. Thief is still a fantastic game to play.

With so many incredible old games to choose from, picking up this list was really tricky. There are several other games which you can try out like Ultima 7, Warcraft II and III, Deus Ex and so on.
